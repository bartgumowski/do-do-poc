What is Do-Do?

Do-Do is a coordination app for two people who share responsibilities and need to stay in sync. Co-parents, couples, flatmates, caregivers - anyone managing a shared life.

Everything lives in one place: your shared task board, calendar, shopping list, expenses, and messages. And because it all updates in real time, you'll both always see the latest version.

Getting started

Create your account

Go to do-do.app and sign in with Google, or create an account with your email and a password. That's it - no app to install (though you can add it to your home screen later).

Invite your partner

Once you're in, head to Settings and look for the Co-parent / Partner section. Enter their email and tap Send invite. They'll get an email with a link - when they click it, you're connected and all your shared content will sync automatically.

💡 Your 14-day free trial starts when you create your account. No credit card needed upfront.

Add your household members

In Settings, you can add your children or pets by name. This makes it easy to reference them when creating cards, expenses, or calendar events.

Install on your home screen (optional but great)

  1. Open Do-Do in your mobile browser
  2. On iOS: tap the Share button, then "Add to Home Screen"
  3. On Android: tap the three-dot menu, then "Add to Home Screen"
  4. Done - it opens like a native app from now on

Board

The Board is your shared to-do list, laid out as three columns. Think of it as a place where you can propose things, track what each person is working on, and mark things done.

The three columns

ColumnWhat it means
To decideSomething needs a decision or agreement before it can move forward. Both of you need to weigh in.
MineYou've taken ownership of this. It's on your plate and you're working on it.
DoneFinished! Completed cards live here so you have a record.

Creating a card

Tap the + button. You can type naturally - Do-Do's AI will pick up dates, times, and who it involves automatically. For example, typing "dentist appointment for Ava on Friday at 3pm" will prefill the title, date, and topic for you.

What's on a card

  • Title - what it's about
  • Topic - Schedule, School, Medical, Expenses, or General
  • Assigned to - you, your partner, or both
  • Due date - optional but useful for reminders
  • Reminder - get notified 15 min, 1 hour, 1 day, or more before it's due
  • Notes / messages - leave a comment or context for your partner
  • Payment - attach a cost that can be paid via the app
  • Receipt - attach a photo for expense tracking

Completing a card

Tap Done on a card when it's finished. It moves to the Done column so you both know it's taken care of. You can always find it there if you need to look back.

Quick actions

On any card you'll see two buttons: "I'll do it" (assign it to yourself) and "Please do it" (assign it to your partner). Great for quickly claiming or delegating without opening the full card.

💡 If you and your partner have conflicting cards - like two events at the same time - Do-Do will flag them and suggest how to resolve the conflict.
⚠️ Cards marked "Can't" are archived rather than deleted - you can still find them if you need the history.

Calendar

The Calendar gives you and your partner a shared view of what's coming up. It can sync with Google Calendar and Apple Calendar so everything stays in one place - no more "wait, when was that?"

Connecting your calendar

Go to Settings > Integrations and connect Google Calendar or Apple CalDAV. Once connected, events sync both ways: things you add in Do-Do appear in your calendar app, and your calendar events appear in Do-Do.

💡 You can connect a work calendar too, and choose to show it as "busy" only - so your partner sees you're unavailable without seeing the details of your work meetings.

Parenting schedule (custody)

If you co-parent, turn on the Parenting schedule in the calendar header or in Settings. Choose your schedule type:

Schedule typePattern
7-7One week with each parent, alternating
2-2-32 days / 2 days / 3 days rotating pattern
5-2Weekdays with one parent, weekends with the other

The calendar then colour-codes each day so it's obvious at a glance who has the children. You can pick your own colour for "my days" and your partner's days.

Day overrides and split days

Plans change. Tap any day in the agenda panel to override who has the kids that day - or tap Split to enter a handover time if the children are with each parent for part of the day. Overridden days show a small badge so you always know when a day differs from the usual pattern.

Conflict alerts

If two events clash, you'll see an alert directly on the calendar. Do-Do will also suggest a possible resolution - you can accept, ignore, or discuss it in Messages.

Importing a Google Calendar as cards

You can pull events from any Google Calendar directly onto your Do-Do board as cards. This is perfect for a shared family calendar, a school calendar, or any calendar you want to see alongside your regular tasks.

To set it up, go to Settings > Calendar connections > Import from Google Calendar and:

  1. Pick a calendar from the dropdown - your Google calendars load automatically.
  2. Choose how many days ahead to import (for example 30 or 90 days).
  3. Pick a sync mode: Import only (read events as cards) or Two-way sync (edits to the card also update the calendar event).
  4. Tap Import now.

After the first import, Do-Do re-syncs automatically every time you open the app - no manual refresh needed.

Imported cards appear on the board mixed with your regular cards and show a small teal globe badge with the calendar name so you can tell them apart at a glance.

💡 You can also turn this on during initial setup - check Import from Google Calendar in the Calendar step and you'll be taken straight to Settings to pick the calendar after finishing setup.

Messages

Messages in Do-Do are organised by topic rather than one long chat. This makes it much easier to find what you're looking for - no more scrolling back through everything to find that dentist appointment detail.

Topics

TopicWhat to put here
📅 SchedulePickup times, drop-offs, changes to the weekly routine
🎒 SchoolPermission slips, parent evenings, teacher updates
🏥 MedicalAppointments, medications, health updates
💶 ExpensesBills to split, payments owed, receipts
💬 GeneralAnything else - day-to-day coordination

Sending a message

Pick a topic, type your message, and tap send. Your partner gets a notification (if they have notifications turned on). Messages update in real time - you'll see replies appear as soon as they're sent.

💡 Attach photos directly in messages - handy for sending a photo of a school letter or a receipt.

Shopping

The shopping list is shared and syncs in real time. If your partner adds something while you're already at the supermarket, it'll appear on your screen immediately.

Two lists

There are two lists - Groceries and Other. Keep food and household basics in Groceries, and one-off purchases (Leo's football socks, that birthday card) in Other.

Using the list

  1. Type an item in the box at the top and tap Add
  2. Tick items as you put them in the trolley - they move to the bottom with a strikethrough
  3. Tap Clear bought when you're done to clean up the list for next time
💡 Bought items stay visible until you clear them - that way your partner can see what's already been picked up even if they arrive at the shop after you.
💶

Expenses

The Expenses section helps you keep track of shared costs and who owes what. No more spreadsheets or awkward "did you pay me back for that?" conversations.

Adding an expense

  1. Tap the + button in Expenses
  2. Enter the amount, what it was for, and who paid
  3. Choose how to split it (50/50, or a custom amount)
  4. Attach a receipt photo if you have one

The balance

At the top of Expenses you'll always see who owes what. It summarises all outstanding costs into a single balance - either "they owe you" or "you owe them" - so you're never confused about where things stand.

Requesting payment

When it's time to settle up, tap Request payment. Your partner gets a link to a payment page where they can pay by card, Apple Pay, or Google Pay - without needing a Do-Do account.

⚠️ Payments are processed securely by Stripe. Do-Do never stores your card details.

Settings

Notifications

Turn on push notifications to get alerts for reminders, new messages, and payment requests. You can set quiet hours so you're not disturbed at night. There's also an option to receive reminders by email if you prefer.

Reminders are smart about who they notify - if a card is assigned to one parent, only that parent gets the alert. Cards assigned to both parents (or unassigned) notify everyone in the family workspace.

Reminder delivery

In Settings > Automation you can choose how reminder alerts are delivered:

ModeWhat happens
App onlyDo-Do sends the push or email notification. Google Calendar is not involved.
Family calendar onlyThe reminder fires as a Google Calendar alert. No in-app notification.
Calendar + Do-DoBoth - you get the Google Calendar alert and the in-app notification.

Language

Do-Do is available in English, German, and Polish. Change the language in Settings > Appearance. The whole app switches immediately.

Theme

Choose Light, Dark, or System (follows your phone's setting).

Calendar integrations

Connect Google Calendar or Apple CalDAV here. You can also connect a separate work calendar and control how much your partner sees (just "busy" times, or full event details).

Frequently asked questions

Does my partner need an account?

Yes - they'll need to create a free account when they accept your invite. Once connected, everything syncs between you automatically.

What happens if I'm offline?

Do-Do works offline. Changes you make are saved locally and sync automatically when you're back online.

Is my data private?

Yes. Your data is stored in the EU (Ireland), and only you and your connected partner can see it. Do-Do uses row-level security to ensure your data is completely isolated from other users. Full details are in the Privacy Policy.

Can I use Do-Do if I'm not a co-parent?

Absolutely. The app works equally well for couples, flatmates, or any two people who share responsibilities. The custody calendar is optional and can be ignored if it's not relevant.

How do I cancel my subscription?

Go to Settings > Subscription and tap Manage subscription. This opens the Stripe billing portal where you can cancel, change plan, or update your payment method.

I accidentally deleted something - can I get it back?

Cards marked "Can't" are archived rather than permanently deleted. For other content, contact support and we can check what's recoverable.

Can I change the language?

Yes - go to Settings > Appearance > Language. Choose from English, German (Deutsch), or Polish (Polski). More languages are on the way.
